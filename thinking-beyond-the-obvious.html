Thinking Beyond The Obvious

The Importance Of Testing What Software Doesn't Do

In software development much of the focus on testing is often placed on ensuring that software does what it's supposed to and we can sometimes easily overlook the idea of having to consider what it doesn't do.

Focusing solely on what a feature is designed to do may lead teams to overlook the unintended consequences of their changes, or fail to account for how other parts of the system may be affected. It can be very easy to forgot to explore the importance of testing both the expected functionality as well as the unintended side effects. A broader approach to testing is sometimes needed that encourages team members to think more critically about the impact of the changes they’re making or requesting.

This wider appraoch to testing encourages a broader, more holistic view of changes and their potential impact and rather than treating individual changes as isolated, all team members should be thinking about how those changes interact with the rest of the system. Every feature, action, or update in software development lives within a broader context and it’s essential to consider that context when designing and executing tests.

Defending Against the Unexpected

Strategies for defensive testing can include:

Negative Testing Negative testing is an essential part of ensuring a system’s resilience. It involves intentionally testing invalid inputs, boundary conditions, or unexpected user behavior to make sure the system reacts appropriately.

Exploration Testing Exploratory testing is a powerful tool, uncovering unexpected behaviors and edge cases that might be missed with conventional testing. By simulating real-world user interactions and thinking critically about potential system weaknesses, exploratory testing helps identify both known and unknown risks. This approach allows teams to anticipate unintended outcomes and design defensive tests that cover not just what the software should do, but also what it shouldn't do.

Regression Testing Every change runs the risk of breaking existing functionality and regression tests confirm that previously working parts of the application continue to work as expected after new changes are introduced. Comprehensive regression tests are a safeguard against unintended side effects.

System Testing While unit and integration tests ensure individual components function correctly, system testing verifies that the entire system works together as expected. System tests are invaluable for catching unexpected interactions between different parts of the system that may not be apparent when testing components in isolation.

Encouraging Thinking in Detail

A key goal of this broader testing philosophy is to get team members to think about the full impact of the changes they’re making or requesting. Too often, teams focus only on the direct outcomes of a change, failing to consider the indirect consequences. By broadening the scope of testing to include what the system shouldn’t do, team members are encouraged to take a more system-wide view of the product.

The outcomes might not always be measurable, however they are obvious:

Improved code: By thinking critically about both what should and shouldn’t happen, team members produce code that is less prone to unexpected issues.

Fewer production issues: Proactively testing for unintended side effects prevents problems from reaching production, reducing firefighting and emergency fixes.

A deeper understanding of the system: Thinking beyond the immediate change encourages team members to understand how their changes fit within the broader system, promoting a more holistic approach to development.

More robust features: Features built with defensive testing are more resilient, secure, and maintainable, leading to a higher-quality product overall.

Never Don't Give Up

Testing software isn’t just about verifying that a feature does what it’s supposed to. It’s also about making sure the feature doesn’t do what it’s not supposed to. By adopting this broader testing mindset, teams can prevent unintended behaviors, reduce risk, and ensure that changes integrate smoothly into the larger system without creating new problems.

Encouraging team members to think in more detail about the full impact of their changes beyond the obvious not only improves the quality of the software but also fosters a culture of thoughtful, responsible development.
